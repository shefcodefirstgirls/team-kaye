Switzerland
Traveller : Kaylee

Travel Information
Flew From: Manchester, UK
Flew Into: Geneva, Switzerland
Purpose of the visit: Research

Hi guys!
I got the chance to visit Switzerland in September 2017 and thought it would be wonderful to tell you about some of the places I visited along the way!

Leg One - The Obergoms
After collecting our hire car from the airport we drove to a litle village located in the canton of Valais. Our first destination was Oberwald, a small village located in Obergoms. This is a beautiful village full of traditional Swiss housing. One house even had cows in a pasture at the front fo the house!
Food
The first night we went to eat at a small resturant which was part of a hotel in the area called the Hotel Alphenhof. Their Mushroom schnitzel is to diiiiie for! The rest of the time we bought food from a local 'Volg' which is a little convinience store. This was the mot cost effective way of eating.
Accomodation
We stayed at the 'Sport and Holiday Camp'. The rooms were in big huts which were raised from the ground. Each block had 6 twin rooms Four shared bathrooms and a shared kitchen/diner.
Activities
Glaciers - There are a number of glaciers you can visit, two of which I was able to get to!
  1. Griesgletscher is a glacier with a hydroelectic dam at its terminus. You can park in a small car park off of the Nufenenstrasse and walk 40 minutes to the dam. From there there are a number of walking trails which boared the Italian/Swiss border. You can actually walk between the two in one day!
  2. Rhone Gletscher is another glacier which is further along the Furkastrasse. There are actually two ways of accessing the glacier. One is to pay at the Belvedere Rhonegletscher which will get you access directly to the terminus of the glacier. The second option is to park further up the road and there is a trail which will tak you up above the glacier, giving you a great view for free!
Whilst visiting Rhone why not go and take a look at the ParcplatzHotel Glacieer du Rhone Museum. They have some great images of the local area.
If you stay in the Obergoms area there are hundreds of walking, hiking and cycling routes which will keep anyone busy, from beginner to the advtanced hiker. I'm not a particularly fit person but managed to walk to some pretty amazing views!
If you decide to go for a hike along the Rotentalhorn glacial hike trail (on the Siwss and Italian boarder) then I'd suggest nipping in for a drink, or even a nights stay, at Crono Gries. its a little hotel situated about 40 mintes walk from Grieslgetscher. The views are beautiful and you couldn't get a more peaceful spot.

Leg Two - Zermatt
After three nights stay in Obergoms we decided to head towards Zermatt.
Accomodation
Due the cost of living in Switzerland being so high, you are best staying in hostels as these are the cheapest types of accomodation available. We stayed in the 'Zermatt' hostel. The staff were lovely and it even had its own bar!
Food
There are plenty of places to eat in Zermatt, all pricey, but all worth it! There is a cute little british pub which do snacks! Lovely bit of 'home away from home'. We also ate at an Italian resturant next to the tain station which did the best calzone I have EVER had. Period.
Activities
You cannot go to Switzerland and not sample the many ... many pubs available. We even found a group of Scotish musicians in one. You never know what you might find!

Leg Three - The Fluhalp
Having enjoyed a cozy three days in costly Zermatt, the three of us decided it was time to move on to 'higher ground. We took the train up toward our final destination of the trip, The FLuhalp. This is a little hotel based in the mountains. after a super cool train journey through (yes ... through!!) the mountain and two cable cars, we were at about 2,000 meters above sea level! We were lucky enough to catch a ride because of the amount of fieldwork equipment we had, however you usually enjoy a 2 hour hike before finally coming across the Fluhalp.
Accomodation
We stayed at The FLuhalp which is about 2,600 meters above sea level! There is plenty to see and do there, but the views are just amazing!
Activities
This is a hikers paradise. With incredible views of the Matterhorn there are a fair few glacial trails to follow. We only went to Findelengletscher as this was where we were conducting out fieldwork, however, I will be back to complete some of the other walks!
Food
Am afraid there are not Volgs at 2,600 meters above sea level, so you don't have alot of choice for food. Lucky the Fluhalp provide 3 square meals a day!

Well, thanks for giving my blog a read! If you made it this far ... you deserve a big gold star!! Switzerland is just amazing and I honestly hope you get a change to experience it first hand! In the meantime, take a look at a few of the photos I took whilst out there in the slideshow below!
Until next time ...

Lots of love,
Kaylee
